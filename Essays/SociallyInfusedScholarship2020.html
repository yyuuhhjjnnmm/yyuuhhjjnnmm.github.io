Essay Prompt: "How will digital marketing change in the 2020s?"

Author: Chujun (Viktor) Zhou

INTRODUCTION

Digital Marketing (D.M.) in the 2020s will change in two profound ways. Firstly, D.M. will become the most important medium for marketing, which means having a dominant digital presence will be critical for successful D.M. in the 2020s. Secondly, companies will need to engage their consumer base by having bespoke and granular advertising as well as robust customer service. In the 2020s, for companies to remain competitive, they will have to dominate the digital landscape and connect to their audience by building a reputation of trustworthiness.

PART 1: DIGITAL DOMINANCE & SEO

The most drastic change to D.M. in the 2020s will be that it will become the most important medium for marketing. With the internet, smartphones, social media, and ecommerce playing a larger role in the daily lives of consumers, companies must develop and maintain a dominant digital presence in the 2020s.

In an article published by Socially Infused (S.I.), a digital marketing firm based in Hamilton, Ontario, the company revealed a list of proven methods to promote content:

  • leverage media outlets and content aggregators
  • use social media groups to personalize content
  • answer questions on Quora and other Q&A websites
  • collaborate with influencers and industry partners[1]

From this list, it is evident that D.M. is one of the most effective and proven methods for  promoting content. Within the same article, it was stated that "in the age of the internet, distributing and promoting your content is almost as important as the content itself".[2] As we can see, digital presence will be critical for companies and their D.M.. In an article by The Economist, the magazine expressed the idea that digital ads are the new rent; as stores abandon brick and mortar locations, they now have the burden to be digitally present. Later in the article, the Economist strengthened their argument by stating that mobile screens capture the majority of consumer attention and going forward, "...most of [the marketing budget] will for the first time go online."[3]

With the rise of the internet, it will be important for companies to harness the power of search engine optimization (SEO) in order to dominate digitally. In another article from S.I., the firm explained that by using SEO, a company can ensure the widespread of their content[4] and establish  "... a sense of trust, creditability, and professionalism." Not only that, but S.I. also discussed the financial benefit of SEO, by stating that despite the big upfront capital expense, it will be the best return on investment a company can get for their marketing dollars.[5]

In summary, D.M. in the 2020s will  become the most important marketing platform for companies worldwide. In order to harness the power of D.M., companies will have to strengthen their digital presence by investing heavily into SEO. Ultimately, only the companies that learn to appreciate the D.M. landscape and invest heavily into their digital presence will prosper in the next decade.

PART 2: DIGITAL BRANDING

In the 2020s, D.M. will progress towards bespoke advertising and focus on the full shopping experience. This encompasses:

  1. targeted advertising
  2. personalized marketing via influencers, social media, reviews and testimonials
  3. receptive customer service

With these three pillars, D.M. will evolve into an arms race of creating the best brand image possible. And by cultivating a great brand image, companies will be able to leverage it along with a dominant digital presence in order to truly synergize their D.M..

With the first pillar, companies can utilize the rise of internet and social media, in order to cater to specific demographics. According to an article from the Economist, advertisers prefer digital platforms because of the available data on age, sex, and interests, which can be leveraged for strategic D.M. campaigns. In the same article, the magazine made an example of Lowe's, a North American hardware store chain, who ran different ads on Facebook depending on the different rooms of a house (kitchen, washroom, bedroom, etc.) that users mentioned in their social media.[6] As we can see, tailoring advertisements has enabled companies to micro-target their audience with customized D.M.. Furthermore, there exists other methods of targeting. In an article by the Digital Marketing Institute, they outlined another targeted D.M. tactic called micro-timing. Micro-timing is achieved by running specific ads that are time sensitive and try to upsell. For example, running an ad for sponges when the customer already has dish soap in their cart. With micro-targeting and micro-timing, companies have been able to maximize the effectiveness of converting leads in sales, but more importantly improve their brand image amongst consumers through D.M.. [7]

But targeted ads alone will not suffice. As the age of technology and internet grows, consumers have been relying more and more on the web to make informed purchases. This means that D.M. will have to follow by forming partnerships with influencers on social media as well as other industry partners to effectively penetrate the consumer base. Not only this, but companies providing a good quality product and service have also been flourishing from honest reviews. Websites such as Yelp, Google Maps reviews as well as ecommerce reviews from platforms like Amazon and eBay have helped companies stand out in an increasingly competitive environment. All of this has been utilized to strengthen the image of a company.

Lastly, as the information flows freely and consumers become more informed, the best way a firm can stand out from the crowd is by providing immaculate customer service. This will involve engaging with their consumer-base on social media, as well as being responsive to feedback. One of the biggest trends outlined by the Digital Marketing Institute is incorporating chatbots and using AI as a part of customer service. This allows companies to streamline customer service and will improve a company's branding immensely by providing responsive customer support.[8]

By effectively equipping these three D.M. strategies, firms will be able to give their digital image a full makeover. This is important, as echoed by S.I. with a short and simple quote: "don't buy [website] links, create relationships". Only by connecting with their customers can a brand hope to build a strong foundation for their brand. And the need for a strong brand will be ever important in D.M. in the 2020s.[9]

CONCLUSION

In the upcoming decade, D.M. will change in two fundamental ways. First, it will become the most important platform for companies, and with that, it will be imperative to invest in SEO. Second, D.M. will shift to focus onto the customers, this means that ads will have to be personalized, firms will have to enlist social media and influencers, companies will have to increase transparency through customer reviews and finally it will be crucial to leverage technology to provide customer service. Although no one has a crystal ball, these two changes are almost guaranteed given how society has adopted technology, and only by anticipating these changes and planning for the future will companies then be able to flourish in the 2020s.
